📝 Scripts & Sequences 101
20 min Study ▶

Scripts are your main weapon. They are pre-written message chains that predict fan responses. 80% of your messages come from scripts. Learn what they are and how to use them.

What Are Scripts?

  • Pre-written message sequences. Each script has multiple steps that guide a conversation toward a goal.
  • Why scripts: They maximize sales, reduce mental load, keep you consistent. You don't invent messages from scratch.
  • The 80/20 rule: 80% scripts, 20% freestyle. Scripts are the base — you personalize the last 20% (using fan's name, details, model's voice).

How to Activate Scripts

  • Type /commandname in Infloww to load a script
  • Examples: /n for new sub, /s for sexting, /price for price objection, /nr for no response
  • The script loads as a template — you read it, personalize it, then send it

Types of Scripts

  • Journey scripts — Welcome (W), Rapport (R), Teasing Bridge (TB), Sexting (S), Aftercare (AC)
  • Objection scripts (OBJ) — Handle "too expensive," "no money," "maybe later"
  • Resistance scripts (RES) — Fan won't go sexual, fan is skeptical
  • Situational scripts (SIT) — Birthday, holidays, special events
  • No Response scripts (NR) — Fan went silent after a PPV drop

Sequencing vs GFE

✔ Sequencing (What We Do)
Follow scripts step by step. Predictable, efficient, scalable.
→ More fans served, less burnout, higher earnings.
"I serve 30 fans per shift and hit my targets consistently."
❌ GFE (Girlfriend Experience)
Chatting freestyle without scripts. Feels natural but...
→ Slower, drains energy, inconsistent results.
"I chatted with 5 fans all shift and only made 1 sale."

🛡 Week 2: Advanced Objection Handling
30 min Study ▶

In the Advanced section, you learned the top 10 objection scripts. Now it's time to go deeper. There are 43 objection scripts across 3 categories. Mastering all of them will make you unstoppable.

The 3 Objection Categories

  • OBJ — Payment Resistance: Fan pushes back on paying. Examples: /price, /expensive, /nomoney, /freecontent, /discount
  • RES — Flow Resistance: Fan resists moving to the next phase. Examples: /nogosexualfan, /notready, /shy
  • SIT — Situational: External factors. Examples: /cardnowork, /busy, /girlfriend, /meeting

12 Core Techniques

  • Reframe: Change how the fan sees the price ("This isn't a cost, it's an experience just for you")
  • Takeaway: Pretend to withdraw the offer ("Okay, maybe it's not for everyone...")
  • Guilt/Investment: Reference the connection built ("After everything we've shared...")
  • Challenge (Competence): "I thought you could handle this..."
  • Challenge (Worthiness): "Only my most special fans get this..."
  • Challenge (Courage): "Are you brave enough to see what happens next?"
  • Downgrade: Offer a smaller package if price is the real issue
  • Social Proof: "Other fans loved this..."
  • FOMO (Temporal): "This offer is only available today..."
  • FOMO (Content): "I only made 3 of these..."
  • Seed & Return: Plant the idea now, revisit later
  • Pay What You Want: Last resort — "How much can you do, babe?"

Key Principles

  • Never argue — always redirect
  • Loss aversion beats gain framing (what they'll MISS is stronger than what they'll GET)
  • Ego is the most powerful lever — make them feel special
  • Alternate positive and negative emotions for maximum impact
  • Commitment & consistency: once they agree to something small, they'll agree to more
💰 Every conversation has one goal — a sale. Objection handling is HOW you get past the "no" to reach the "yes."

Mirroring

Mirroring means reflecting the fan’s energy, language, and style back to them. When you mirror someone, they unconsciously feel understood and connected.

  • Match the fan’s energy level — hyped fan? Be hyped back. Chill fan? Be chill.
  • Match their vocabulary — if they say “mi amor”, you say “mi amor” back
  • Match their emoji usage — if they use lots of emojis, you use lots. If none, keep yours minimal.
  • If they’re naughty and direct — be naughty and direct back
  • If they’re sweet and romantic — be sweet and romantic back
  • Mirroring builds unconscious rapport — they feel “she really gets me”
