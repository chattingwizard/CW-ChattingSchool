📝 Scripts & Sequences 101
20 min Study ▶

Scripts are your main weapon. They are pre-written message chains that predict fan responses. 80% of your messages come from scripts. Learn what they are and how to use them.

Key Points

  • What scripts are: Pre-written message sequences. Each script has multiple steps that guide a conversation.
  • Why scripts: Maximize sales, reduce mental load, stay consistent. You don't need to invent messages from scratch.
  • The 80/20 rule: 80% scripts, 20% freestyle. Scripts are the base — you personalize the last 20%.
  • How to activate: Type /commandname in Infloww (e.g., /n for new sub, /s for sexting, /price for price objection)
  • Script types:
  • • Journey scripts — Welcome (W), Rapport (R), Teasing Bridge (TB), Sexting (S), Aftercare (AC)
  • • Objection scripts (OBJ) — Handle price complaints, "no money," etc.
  • • Resistance scripts (RES) — Fan won't go sexual, fan is skeptical
  • • Situational scripts (SIT) — Birthday, holidays, special events
  • • No Response scripts (NR) — Fan went silent after a PPV drop
  • Sequencing vs GFE: GFE (Girlfriend Experience) means chatting freely. Sequencing means following scripts systematically. Sequencing wins — more fans served, less burnout, higher earnings.

Sequence A series of scripts connected together to move a fan through the journey
Command What you type to activate a script (e.g., /n, /s, /price)
80/20 Rule 80% scripts, 20% freestyle personalization
GFE Girlfriend Experience — chatting freely without scripts (less efficient)

🎨 Custom Content Sales (TPDs)
15 min Study ▶

Customs are high-value sales — photos from $50+, videos from $150–200+. Learn the TPDs method to sell them naturally.

The TPDs Method

  • T — Teasing: Build excitement before dropping the price. "Mmm I'd love to make that just for you... imagine me doing exactly what you want"
  • P — Price: Drop the price while the fan is excited. "That would be $150 babe, and I'll make it so worth it"
  • Ds — Distraction Shift: Immediately ask a question to redirect attention away from the price. "So tell me... what would you want me to wear?"

Key Rules

  • NEVER give exact delivery time — say "I'll make it as soon as I can, babe"
  • ALWAYS collect payment BEFORE the custom is made
  • Log all custom orders in fan notes for TL coordination

When to Pitch

  • During aftercare — fan is emotionally connected
  • When a fan mentions a fantasy — "I could make that just for you..."
  • When a fan asks directly — then follow TPDs
